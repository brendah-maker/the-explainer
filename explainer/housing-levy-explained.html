Housing Levy Explained: What It Means for Employees and Employers in Kenya

Published March 2026

The Housing Levy is a relatively recent initiative introduced by the Kenyan government with the goal of funding affordable housing projects across the country. This policy mandates contributions from both employees and employers, providing a structured mechanism to mobilize resources for housing development while simultaneously creating a predictable source of financing for construction of affordable homes.

Understanding the Housing Levy

At its core, the Housing Levy is a mandatory deduction applied to an employee's gross salary. Employees contribute 1.5% of their basic salary, and this contribution is matched by the employer, effectively making the total contribution 3% of the employee's salary.

Example: If an employee earns **KSh50,000**, the employee contributes **KSh750** and the employer contributes **KSh750**, making **KSh1,500 total monthly**.

How Contributions Are Calculated

Calculating the Housing Levy is straightforward once the salary is known. These deductions are collected monthly alongside other statutory deductions and remitted to the government housing fund responsible for financing affordable housing projects.

Implications for Employees

From an employee's perspective, the levy slightly reduces take-home income. However, the program aims to expand access to affordable housing, potentially enabling contributors to benefit from housing schemes or subsidized mortgages in the future.

Implications for Employers

Employers must match the employee contribution and ensure payroll systems are configured correctly. Compliance is important, as failure to remit contributions may lead to penalties.

Why the Government Introduced the Housing Levy

Kenya faces a large housing deficit, particularly for low- and middle-income earners. The levy was introduced as a sustainable financing mechanism to increase housing supply while also stimulating the construction sector and creating jobs.

Conclusion

The Housing Levy represents a policy designed to address Kenya's housing shortage through collective contributions from employees and employers. If implemented effectively, it could significantly expand access to affordable housing while supporting economic growth through the construction sector.
