Raphael Tuju’s KSh 2.2 Billion Loan Dispute Reaches Breaking Point

Former Cabinet Secretary Raphael Tuju is at the center of a dramatic escalation in a decade-long legal battle over a multibillion-shilling loan. In March 2026, the dispute reached a critical point following a controversial eviction from his Karen property and a series of court setbacks.

Midnight Eviction Drama

On March 14, 2026, at around 3:00 AM, more than 50 police officers from the Rapid Response Unit (RRU) reportedly stormed Dari Business Park in Karen. The operation saw security guards and staff forcibly removed, with the premises sealed off.

The move disrupted approximately 25 businesses operating within the compound, leaving them inaccessible. Tuju has strongly contested the eviction, claiming it was carried out without a valid court order and describing it as an example of “the law of the jungle.”

In defiance, Tuju vowed not to leave the 27-acre property, stating that those attempting to remove him would have to “kill him first.”

The Loan Dispute Explained

The conflict traces back to 2015, when Dari Limited secured a $9.3 million loan from the East African Development Bank (EADB) to fund hospitality developments. Over time, interest and penalties caused the debt to balloon significantly.

  • Original loan: $9.3 million
  • Current estimates: Between KSh 2.2 billion and KSh 4.5 billion

On March 9, 2026, the High Court dismissed Tuju’s attempt to block the auction of the property, with the judge describing the case as a “blatant abuse of court process.”

Auction and Ownership Changes

Court documents indicate that the property was auctioned for approximately KSh 450 million. The reported buyer is Ultra Eureka Limited, a company associated with businessman Jackson Chebet.

This development effectively transferred ownership of the property, intensifying the standoff between Tuju and authorities.

Contempt of Court and Legal Pressure

Earlier in March 2026, Tuju and his three children were fined KSh 100,000 each for contempt of court. The ruling stemmed from allegations that they obstructed court-appointed receiver-managers in 2020.

These legal setbacks have further weakened Tuju’s position in the long-running dispute.

Political and Corruption Allegations

Tuju has made serious allegations, claiming that powerful government figures are behind efforts to take over his property. He has specifically accused President William Ruto’s allies of orchestrating the eviction using state machinery.

Additionally, Tuju petitioned Chief Justice Martha Koome, alleging that he was pressured to pay a KSh 10 million bribe to influence the outcome of the case.

What This Means

The case highlights the complex intersection of business, law, and politics in Kenya. It raises questions about:

  • Property rights and enforcement of court orders
  • The role of financial institutions in loan recovery
  • Allegations of political interference in legal disputes

As investigations and legal proceedings continue, the outcome of this case could set an important precedent for high-value property disputes in Kenya.
