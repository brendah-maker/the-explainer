How Fuel Prices Are Determined in Kenya

Published March 2026 • 5 min read

In Kenya, fuel prices are regulated monthly by the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA). The process ensures transparency, fairness, and national consistency, protecting consumers from sudden price shocks.

Factors that influence fuel prices in Kenya: • Global crude oil prices and exchange rates • Government taxes and levies (excise, petroleum levy, road levy) • Logistics and distribution costs • Regulated margins for oil companies and retailers • Incidental costs like storage losses and port demurrage

Key Components of the Fuel Pricing Formula

EPRA’s pricing formula calculates maximum wholesale and retail prices for petroleum products, considering:

  • Landed Cost: Cost of importing fuel, influenced by global prices and the USD/KSh exchange rate.
  • Government Taxes and Levies: Includes excise duty, Petroleum Development Levy, and Road Maintenance Levy.
  • Logistics and Distribution: Costs of storage, port handling, and transportation to retail stations.
  • Regulated Margins: Fixed margins for oil marketing companies (OMCs) and retailers to cover operating costs.
  • Incidental Costs: Includes demurrage penalties and allowable storage losses.

The Monthly Pricing Process

EPRA follows a structured monthly process:

  • Monthly Review: On the 14th, new maximum prices for petrol, diesel, and kerosene are calculated and take effect on the 15th.
  • Weighted Average Calculation: Prices are based on the weighted average cost of fuel cargoes imported and discharged at the Port of Mombasa during a set period.
  • Regulatory Oversight: Margins and costs are monitored to ensure fair and predictable pricing.

Why This Approach Matters

Kenya’s regulated pricing ensures that fuel prices reflect actual costs while maintaining fairness for consumers and businesses. It also allows policymakers to fund critical infrastructure and road maintenance without exposing citizens to extreme volatility.

Conclusion

Understanding how fuel prices are determined helps citizens and businesses plan for monthly expenses. EPRA’s formula balances global, domestic, and logistical costs, providing a transparent and consistent approach to petroleum pricing.
