How M-PESA Works

Published March 2026

M-PESA ("M" for mobile, "PESA" for money in Swahili) is a mobile-phone-based, SMS-driven, and app-based money transfer and microfinancing service. It functions as a digital wallet linked to a user's mobile number, allowing deposits, withdrawals, money transfers, and bill payments without a traditional bank account. M-PESA uses a network of agents to reach both urban and rural populations.

Registration

Users register at an authorized M-PESA agent, such as a retail outlet, airtime reseller, or Safaricom shop. Registration links the M-PESA account to the mobile number and requires a personal PIN for secure transactions.

Deposit (Cash-in)

Users deposit physical cash with an agent, which is converted into electronic money (e-money) in the mobile wallet. This balance can then be used for money transfers, payments, or withdrawals.

Transactions

  • Send Money: Transfer funds to other M-PESA users via SMS or the mobile app, including both registered and unregistered recipients.
  • Pay Bills & Goods: Pay utility bills, school fees, government fees, or merchant purchases using the "Pay Bill" or "Buy Goods" services.
  • Withdrawal (Cash-out): Convert e-money back to cash at any authorized M-PESA agent, ensuring easy access to funds.

Security

Every transaction requires a PIN, providing secure access. Real-time SMS notifications are sent to both sender and recipient, while encrypted channels protect against fraud and unauthorized access.

Technology

M-PESA operates through SIM-card applications or USSD codes (e.g., *334# in Kenya) and processes transactions in real-time via Safaricom’s secure servers. Users can also manage their accounts through the M-PESA mobile app for balance checks, transfers, and payments.

Additional Features

Beyond money transfers, M-PESA provides microloans (M-Shwari, KCB M-PESA), savings accounts, and insurance products. These services allow users to save, access credit, and manage financial risks, making M-PESA a full digital financial ecosystem for the unbanked and underbanked.

Conclusion

M-PESA is a secure, convenient, and accessible mobile money service that has transformed financial inclusion in Kenya. By enabling essential financial transactions without a bank account, it benefits both urban and rural users.
